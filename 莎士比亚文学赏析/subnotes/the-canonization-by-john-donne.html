// Petrarchan Conceit
Call us what you will, we are made such by love;

Call her one, me another fly,

We're tapers too, and at our own cost die,

fly : 飞蛾
taper : 蜡烛

And we in us find the eagle and the dove.

The phœnix riddle hath more wit

By us; we two being one, are it.

So, to one neutral thing both sexes fit.

We die and rise the same, and prove

Mysterious by this love.

We can die by it, if not live by love,

And if unfit for tombs and hearse

Our legend be, it will be fit for verse;

tombs, hearse : for the king or high lord
but we can in verse

And if no piece of chronicle we prove,

We'll build in sonnets pretty rooms;

chronicle : also for high lords
sonnet

As well a well-wrought urn becomes

The greatest ashes, as half-acre tombs,

And by these hymns, all shall approve

Us canonized for Love.

And thus invoke us: "You, whom reverend love

invoke : pray for
reverend : respected

hermitage: 隐居

The Canonization 《封圣》
BY JOHN DONNE 约翰・多恩
For God's sake hold your tongue, and let me love, 看在上帝的份上，住嘴，让我去爱，
Or chide my palsy, or my gout, 要么就骂我的瘫痪、我的痛风，
My five gray hairs, or ruined fortune flout, 嘲笑我的五根白发、我的破败财运，
With wealth your state, your mind with arts improve, 用财富抬高你的地位，用学识充实你的头脑，
Take you a course, get you a place, 谋个前程，混个职位，
Observe his honor, or his grace, 逢迎权贵的尊荣，讨好他们的恩宠，
Or the king's real, or his stampèd face 或是凝视国王的真容，或是他印在钱币上的肖像，
Contemplate; what you will, approve, 随你做什么，随你怎么想，
So you will let me love. 只求你别管我的爱。
Alas, alas, who's injured by my love? 唉，唉，我的爱伤害了谁？
What merchant's ships have my sighs drowned? 我的叹息淹没了哪艘商船？
Who says my tears have overflowed his ground? 谁说我的泪水淹没了他的田地？
When did my colds a forward spring remove? 我的寒意何曾耽误了早来的春光？
When did the heats which my veins fill 我血管里沸腾的爱意
Add one more to the plaguy bill? 何曾为瘟疫名单多添一个名字？
Soldiers find wars, and lawyers find out still 士兵自有战争可打，律师也总能找到
Litigious men, which quarrels move, 爱打官司的人挑起争端，
Though she and I do love. 哪怕我和她相爱。
Call us what you will, we are made such by love; 随你怎么称呼我们，我们因爱而成此模样；
Call her one, me another fly, 称她为一只飞蛾，我为另一只，
We're tapers too, and at our own cost die, 我们也是蜡烛，燃尽自己，为爱而亡，
And we in us find the eagle and the dove. 我们的体内，既有鹰的勇猛，也有鸽的温柔。
The phœnix riddle hath more wit 凤凰的谜题，在我们身上更显智慧：
By us; we two being one, are it. 我们二人合而为一，便是那不死的神鸟。
So, to one neutral thing both sexes fit. 两种性别，融于一个中性的生命。
We die and rise the same, and prove 我们同生同死，以此证明
Mysterious by this love. 这份爱，何其神秘。
We can die by it, if not live by love, 若不能因爱而生，我们便为爱而死，
And if unfit for tombs and hearse 若我们的传奇不配入棺下葬，
Our legend be, it will be fit for verse; 它也定能在诗中永存；
And if no piece of chronicle we prove, 若史书上没有我们的名字，
We'll build in sonnets pretty rooms; 我们便在十四行诗里，筑起华美的殿堂；
As well a well-wrought urn becomes 一只精致的瓮，
The greatest ashes, as half-acre tombs, 足以盛放最尊贵的骨灰，正如半亩大的陵墓，
And by these hymns, all shall approve 而借着这些颂歌，世人终将见证
Us canonized for Love. 我们因爱而被封圣。
And thus invoke us: "You, whom reverend love 后世将如此称颂我们：“你们，被神圣的爱
Made one another's hermitage; 造就了彼此的隐修之所；
You, to whom love was peace, that now is rage; 你们，于爱曾是安宁，如今却为爱燃烧；
Who did the whole world's soul contract, and drove 你们，将整个世界的灵魂浓缩，
Into the glasses of your eyes 驱入你们的眼眸，
(So made such mirrors, and such spies, （化作明镜，化作窥镜，
That they did all to you epitomize) 将世间万物，都缩影于你们身上）
Countries, towns, courts: beg from above 各国、各城、各廷：都向上天祈求，
A pattern of your love!" 赐下你们爱的典范！”
