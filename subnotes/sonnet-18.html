sonnet 18

Shall I compare thee to a summer’s day?
(similie)

Thou art more lovely and more temperate:

Rough winds do shake the darling buds of May,
(personification: summer->tenant)

And summer’s lease hath all too short a date;

Sometime too hot the eye of heaven shines,
(metaphor: the eye of heaven->sun)

And often is his gold complexion dimm'd;
(complextion: 面色，脸色)
(personification: his gold complextion dimm'd->sunshine is too weak)

And every fair from fair sometime declines,
(alliteration: fair from fair; chance or nature's changing course)
(the first fair refers to beauty concept itself and the second fair refers to person)

By chance or nature’s changing course untrimm'd;
(untrimmed未加修饰的)

But thy eternal summer shall not fade,
(metaphor: eternal summer -> eternal beauty)
Nor lose possession of that fair thou ow’st;

Nor shall death brag thou wander’st in his shade,
(brag: 吹嘘)

When in eternal lines to time thou grow’st:
keep your beauty in peom will be as eternal as time
the theme is language, poem will be eternal
So long as men can breathe or eyes can see,

   So long lives this, and this gives life to thee.
So long as -> as long as
So long live this -> so this will live eternal

创作背景

收录于1609年出版的《莎士比亚十四行诗》，共154首；本诗属Fair Youth系列（1–126首），献给一位神秘贵族青年

结构和格律

结构：3个四行联（quatrains）+ 1个双行对句（couplet），共14行
韵律：ABAB CDCD EFEF GG
节奏：抑扬格五音步 Iambic pentameter

The four steps (structure) of Shakespeare sonnet

The four steps (structure) of Shakespeare sonnet
  1. Introduction (opening)
  2. Elucidation of the theme (developing; raising problems)
  3. Transition to another viewpoint (changing)
  4. Summing uo (concluding and may reinforce the theme; give a solution)
